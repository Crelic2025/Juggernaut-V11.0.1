What This App Is

A 16-week powerlifting program tracker that tells you exactly what weight to put on the bar, how many sets and reps, how long to rest, and tracks progress. The program is the Inverted Juggernaut Method 2.0 — many sets of few reps (10×5) instead of few sets of many reps (5×10). Same total work, better form on every rep.

On top of the program, an AI hybrid layer checks how you’re feeling before workouts, adjusts volume if you’re beat up, and gives coaching feedback after sessions.

Settings — Do This First

Program: Set units to lb, bar weight to 45, rounding to 2.5. Pick your 1RM Formula — Epley (default, good for most lifters), Brzycki, or RPE-based (uses the Tuchscherer percentage table). Start with Standard 16-week mode.

Lifts & Maxes: Enter your true 1RM for each lift. The app calculates your Training Max (TM) at 90% automatically.

Barbell Profiles: Below the 1RM editor, set per-lift bar weight, collar weight, rounding, and plate inventory. Useful if you use different bars (e.g. 55 lb squat bar vs 45 lb bench bar). The app uses these for exact plate breakdowns and snaps weights to what you can actually load.

Rest Timers: A wave×phase grid. 10s wave uses short rest (60s). Heavier waves get 2.5 min. "0" = no auto-timer. All overridable.

AI & Readiness: Toggle Daily Readiness check and RPE logging on/off. Paste your Anthropic API key for AI coaching. Optional — the app works fully without it.

UI & Performance: Enable Performance Mode to disable blur effects and animations. Useful on older phones. Switches to system fonts for faster rendering.

Gym Equipment: Six toggles for the equipment you have access to — Barbell, Dumbbells, Cables, Bands, Machines, and Pull-up Bar. All are on by default. Toggle off anything you don't have and the swap chip lists in the exercise help modal will only show alternatives you can actually do. Your default accessories stay unchanged so you don't lose progress tracking — but if an exercise needs equipment you've toggled off, an amber warning appears on the card suggesting you swap. Bodyweight exercises are always available regardless of toggles.

Estimating Your 1RM

The app asks for your one-rep max (1RM) — the heaviest weight you can lift for exactly one rep with good form. If you've never tested this, there are two ways to estimate it:

Option 1 — Use a recent heavy set. If you know you can bench 185 lb for 5 reps, use the calculator tool (tap the calculator icon in the header): enter 185 and 5 reps, and it will estimate your 1RM at about 216 lb. Do this for each lift.

Option 2 — Be conservative. If you genuinely have no idea, use a weight you're confident you could do for 3 slow, controlled reps. That's roughly your 90% — enter it as your 1RM. The Training Max buffer means you'll start light, which is always better than starting too heavy. You can adjust upward after your first realization week when the AMRAP tells you where you actually are.

Don't ego-inflate your numbers. The entire program is built on your 1RM being honest. If you add 20 lb to look good on screen, every prescribed weight will be too heavy, you'll grind ugly reps, miss AMRAP targets, and the progression system won't work. Start conservative — the AMRAP system will self-correct upward fast if your numbers were too low.

The 16-Week Program

Four 4-week waves: 10s (high volume, 10×5), 8s (8×5), 5s (6×5 heavier), 3s (7×3 heaviest). Each wave follows a weekly rhythm:

Week 1 Accumulation: High volume. Build your base.

Week 2 Intensification: Heavier weight, fewer reps. Adapt to load.

Week 3 Realization: Test day. Ramp-up warm-ups then AMRAP. Measures progress.

Week 4 Deload: Recovery. Light weight. Never skip.

The timeline bar shows all 16 weeks — blue glow = current, green = done, faded = upcoming.

Scheduling Your Training Week

You train 4 sessions per week — one lift per session. Each session you pick one of your four lifts (Squat, Bench, Deadlift, OHP), complete the main lift work, do your accessories for that lift, and you're done. A typical split looks like:

Monday: Squat · Tuesday: Bench · Thursday: Deadlift · Friday: OHP

The exact days don't matter as long as you follow two rules: don't train two days in a row if you can avoid it (your body needs recovery between sessions), and separate squat and deadlift by at least two days (they both hammer your lower back and posterior chain). If your schedule only allows 3 days, combine OHP with bench day — do bench as the main lift, then OHP accessories or vice versa.

Each week in the program (Accumulation, Intensification, etc.) means completing all 4 lifts once. The app tracks which lifts you've done via the lift chips on the dashboard — green checkmark means done. When all 4 are checked off, the week is complete and the program advances to the next phase.

Order within the week doesn't matter. Squat first or deadlift first — the app doesn't care. Just do all 4 before the week advances. Most people follow the same order each week for consistency.

Dashboard

When no session is active, the dashboard shows: where you are in the program (wave + phase description), which lifts are done/pending this week (tap any chip to preview), and the full workout preview with weight, plates, scheme, rest time, and percentage. Last Session shows your most recent result for the selected lift.

Timeline Bar

The colored pills at the top show all 16 weeks. Blue glow = current week, green = completed, faded = future. Tap any week to preview that week's weights and scheme for all lifts — useful for planning ahead. Tap "Back to current" to return. You can also tap the Program Overview chart bars for the same preview.

Lift Intelligence

Below the main dashboard, the Lift Intelligence card shows per-lift volume tracking with colored progress bars. Each bar represents your hard sets for the current program week relative to the MEV (Minimum Effective Volume) and MRV (Maximum Recoverable Volume) targets for that lift and phase. "Hard sets" means your working sets from the main lift — ramp/warmup sets are excluded.

Status badges tell you where you stand: Below Target (amber), In Range (green), Near Ceiling (red), or Above Recoverable. Tap any lift to expand coaching details. Recommendations reference your T1 accessories (supplemental barbell variations) as the volume lever — since the main lift sets are prescribed by the program, your accessories are how you adjust total weekly stimulus for each movement pattern.

Weekly Plan

The Weekly Plan card shows how many lifts are done vs. remaining this week, how many days are left, and a pace indicator (On Pace / Tight / Behind). Each lift has a colored dot — green = done, blue = due, grey = skipped. A coach recommendation at the bottom suggests your next priority lift.

Daily Workout Flow

Readiness Check

Tap Start Workout. Rate five factors 1–5: Sleep, Motivation, Nutrition, Soreness, and Performance. The app converts these to a 0–30 readiness score. Below 15 triggers auto-regulation — an autoregulation card explains what changed (e.g. “10 → 9 sets”). Below 8 drops two sets instead of one. Tap “Train Full Volume Anyway” to override.

Quick-Fill: If you trained recently, a “Same as Last (4/3/4/4/3)” button appears at the top of the readiness modal. One tap fills all five factors from your last session — saves time when your condition hasn’t changed much.

A compact readiness mini-bar stays visible throughout the session showing all five factor dots and your score.

Quick-Tap Logging

For working sets where reps match target, tap the set row to instantly log it — green flash confirms. For AMRAP or adjusted reps, tap navigates to the hero card stepper.

The Hero Card

Big weight number, barbell SVG with color-coded plates (red=45, blue=35, green=25, orange=10, purple=5, grey=2.5), target reps, percentage, RIR, estimated 1RM.

RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion)

Purple stepper on AMRAP and realization sets. RPE 8 = 2 reps left. RPE 9 = 1 rep left. RPE 10 = absolute max.

Rest Timer

Bottom-sheet timer with circular countdown + “Up Next” preview showing next set’s weight and plate changes. Close it — purple badge keeps counting in the header.

Session Timer

A running clock appears in the header badge bar when a session is active. Tap the timer badge to pause/resume — the display dims when paused, and paused time is excluded from your final session duration. Useful for water breaks, reracking, or chatting.

Mid-Session Check

After 3 working sets: “How’s the movement feeling?” — Sluggish / Normal / Dialed In. One tap, stored for AI coaching.

Realization Day (Week 3)

Ramp-up sets (50%×5, 60%×3, 70%×2, working%×1) with blue “RAMP” pills. Rate each: ▼ Heavier / → Normal / ▲ Light. Or tap “Quick Log All Warm-Up Sets” to skip all 4.

After ramps, a coaching note appears based on feedback. The Goal 1RM panel calculates required reps for any target max.

Progression

For every rep beyond the wave target, 1RM goes up 5 lb lower body / 2.5 lb upper body, capped at 10 extra reps. Green confetti on PR days.

After the 16 Weeks

When all 16 weeks are complete, you've finished one full cycle of the Inverted Juggernaut Method. Your 1RMs have been updated automatically through the realization AMRAP tests. What now?

Run it again. Go to Settings and reset the program cycle. Your new, higher 1RMs carry over — the app recalculates every weight in the program based on your updated numbers. The same 16-week structure now prescribes heavier weights because your base is stronger. Most serious lifters run 2-4 consecutive Juggernaut cycles before switching programs.

Before restarting, consider taking a full week off or doing a light "bridge" week of general fitness (cardio, mobility, bodyweight circuits). 16 weeks of structured training accumulates fatigue that a single deload week can't fully clear. Coming back fresh for Cycle 2 means better performance from week 1.

Adjust your TM multiplier if needed. If you were consistently hitting AMRAP targets with 3+ reps to spare, keep the multiplier at 1.0. If you were barely hitting targets or missing some, drop it to 0.95 for the next cycle — this gives you a slightly lighter start that builds more momentum through the waves.

AI Coaching

With an Anthropic API key in Settings, Claude Sonnet analyzes your session data — sets, RPE, readiness, warmup feedback, movement quality, volume trends — and returns 2–3 sentences of personalized coaching in a purple card.

System Philosophy

What Is the Juggernaut Method?

The Juggernaut Method was created by Chad Wesley Smith, founder of Juggernaut Training Systems and one of the strongest raw powerlifters in history (905 lb squat, 2200+ lb total). He designed it by combining the wave-loading structure of Soviet periodization with the submaximal training philosophy of Jim Wendler's 5/3/1 and the undulating rep schemes used by elite Olympic weightlifters. The result is a system that builds strength through intelligent volume management rather than grinding maximal weights every session.

The core insight: strength is built by accumulating quality reps at submaximal loads, not by testing your max every week. Most lifters stall because they train too heavy too often, accumulate fatigue faster than they can recover, and never give their body the organized stimulus it needs to adapt. The Juggernaut Method fixes this by controlling exactly how much stress you apply, when you apply it, and when you back off.

What Is the Inverted Juggernaut Method 2.0?

The original Juggernaut Method uses traditional set/rep schemes: 5 sets of 10 reps in the 10s wave, 4 sets of 8 in the 8s wave, etc. The Inverted version flips this: 10 sets of 5 in the 10s wave, 8 sets of 5 in the 8s wave. You do more sets of fewer reps instead of fewer sets of more reps.

Why does this matter? When you do a set of 10 heavy reps, the first 5 reps are crisp and the last 5 are a fatigued grind where form breaks down. With 10 sets of 5, every single rep is in the quality zone. You get the same total volume (50 reps either way) but with dramatically better technique practice, lower per-set fatigue, and less injury risk. This is especially important for powerlifting where the competition lift is a single rep — you want to practice perfect singles and triples, not sloppy sets of 10.

Version 2.0 refines the original with more precise percentage tables, a structured realization (testing) protocol with ramp-up sets, wave-specific rest periods, and a built-in AMRAP progression system that auto-adjusts your training max based on performance.

Who Is This For?

The Juggernaut Method works for intermediate to advanced lifters who have established their basic movement patterns and want a structured, long-term approach to getting stronger. If you can squat, bench, deadlift, and overhead press with reasonable form and you know your approximate 1RM for each, this program will take you further. It's not a peaking program for a meet next month — it's a 16-week system that builds a bigger, stronger base and then tests it. Most lifters run multiple cycles back-to-back, resetting with higher maxes each time.

Why Inverted?

Traditional programs use 5×10 (few sets, many reps). You gas out by rep 7 and grind ugly reps. Inverted flips it: 10×5 (many sets, few reps). Every rep is crisp. You get the same total volume with better technique practice, less fatigue per set, and lower injury risk. Think of it as quality reps over survival reps.

Why Waves?

Your body adapts to specific rep ranges in about 4 weeks, then progress stalls. Waves cycle through high reps → medium → low → heavy so you never plateau. The 10s wave builds muscle and work capacity. The 8s wave bridges to strength. The 5s wave builds raw strength. The 3s wave peaks it. Then you retest and start over with a higher 1RM. Each wave builds on the last.

Why Accumulation → Intensification → Realization → Deload?

Accumulation overloads your body with volume at moderate weight — this is the stimulus that forces adaptation. Intensification drops volume but raises weight, teaching your nervous system to handle heavier loads. Realization tests what you built — one all-out AMRAP set measures your new capacity. Deload lets your body actually absorb the training. Without deloads, fatigue accumulates until you break down. The pattern is: stress → adapt → test → recover.

Why 90% Training Max?

Training at your true max every session leads to burnout, missed reps, and injuries. The TM creates a 10% buffer so every prescribed weight is one you can handle on your worst day. As your AMRAP performance drives the TM up, the buffer shrinks naturally — but you're always progressing on weights you own, not weights that own you.

The 3-Tier Accessory System

Accessories exist to build the muscles that the main lift can't fully develop on its own.

Tier 1 (Supplemental, 5–8 reps): Barbell variations that target your specific weak point. If you fail your squat at the bottom, Pause Squats strengthen that exact position. If your bench stalls at lockout, Close-Grip Bench builds the triceps to push through. T1 exercises use heavy weight and low reps because they're practicing the competition movement pattern. Pick T1s based on where YOUR lift breaks down.

Tier 2 (Hypertrophy, 8–15 reps): Muscle-building exercises that make the prime movers and stabilizers bigger. A bigger muscle has more potential for strength. Rows build the upper back shelf that stabilizes every press. Split squats build single-leg strength that transfers to the squat. T2 exercises should balance push/pull. For every press, you need a pull. The defaults are set up this way — bench T2 leads with a row, not another press.

Tier 3 (Core/Prehab, 10–20 reps): Injury prevention and stability. These won't directly add pounds to your max, but they keep you training consistently. Face pulls protect your shoulders. Ab wheels build the brace that protects your spine. The best ability is availability — you can't get strong if you're injured.

Why Double Progression for Accessories?

Main lifts use periodized percentages that change weekly. Accessories don't need that complexity. Instead, hold the weight constant and build reps. When every set hits the top of the range (e.g., 4×8 all hitting 8), you've proven you own that weight — add the increment and start building again from the bottom. This is simple, self-regulating, and prevents the common mistake of ego-loading accessories at the expense of form.

How the Defaults Are Selected

Each lift's default accessories are chosen to address both sides of the lift — one exercise for the bottom position and one for the top/lockout — plus balanced push/pull in T2 and evidence-based prehab in T3. The categories next to each exercise (e.g., "Weak off chest", "Weak at lockout") tell you when to use it. Tap the ? on any exercise to see alternatives and swap based on your specific needs.

Your Workout: Start to Finish

Here's exactly what a full training session looks like in the app — from opening it at the gym to logging your last accessory set.

Step 1 — Open & Check the Dashboard

When you open the app, the Workout tab shows your current position in the program: the wave (10s/8s/5s/3s), the phase (Accumulation/Intensification/Realization/Deload), and the week number (e.g. "5S · Intensification · Wk 10/16"). Below that you'll see:

Lift chips — colored buttons for each active lift. Green checkmark = done this week. Blue = still due. Tap any chip to select that lift and preview the workout below.

Workout preview — shows the weight, plate breakdown (with barbell SVG), set/rep scheme, rest time, and percentage for the selected lift. This is what you'll be lifting today.

Last Session — your most recent result for this lift: date, reps per set, tonnage, readiness score.

Lift Intelligence — colored progress bars showing your weekly volume vs. MEV/MRV targets. If a bar is amber ("Below Target"), your T1 accessories are the lever to fix it.

Weekly Plan — how many lifts are done vs. due, days remaining, pace badge, and a "Next up" recommendation that factors in squat/deadlift spacing.

Step 2 — Start Workout

Tap the green Start Workout button. If readiness is enabled (default), a modal slides up with five sliders:

Sleep · Motivation · Nutrition · Soreness · Performance — rate each 1–5. If you trained recently, a "Same as Last" quick-fill button appears at the top — one tap fills all five from your last session.

Tap Start. The app calculates your readiness score (0–30). If it's below 15, you'll see an amber Fatigue Managed card explaining that 1 or 2 working sets have been removed (1 set dropped below 15, 2 sets dropped below 8). You can tap "Train Full Volume Anyway" to override this. Deload weeks are never reduced.

The dashboard disappears and the session view takes over.

Step 3 — Working Sets

The hero card dominates the screen: big weight number (64px for gym visibility), a color-coded barbell plate diagram, target reps, percentage of TM, and RIR estimate.

Below the hero card: rep stepper buttons (− and +) to adjust reps if needed, and the action row:

DONE — full-width green button, 64px tall. Tap when the set is complete. This is the button you'll hit most — it's designed for chalk-covered thumbs.

Undo — reverses the last logged set if you tapped by accident.

Rest — opens the rest timer bottom sheet with a circular countdown and "Up Next" preview showing the next set's weight and any plate changes.

A session timer badge appears in the header — tap it to pause/resume (paused time is excluded from your final duration).

The readiness mini-bar stays visible showing your five factor dots and score.

Step 4 — Quick-Tap Logging

For working sets where reps match the target, you don't need the hero card at all. Tap the set row in the set list below — it instantly logs with a green flash. This is the fastest path: tap row → auto-rest timer → tap next row → repeat.

Each set row shows the weight in bold, the TM percentage, and reps completed. When the next set has a different weight, an amber plate change callout appears (e.g. "+12.5 lb change") so you know to adjust the bar.

Step 5 — Mid-Session Check

After 3 working sets, a card appears: "How's the movement feeling?" — tap Sluggish / Normal / Dialed In. One tap, stored for the AI coaching summary. This takes 1 second and helps the app give better feedback.

Step 6 — AMRAP Set (Last Working Set)

The final working set is tagged AMRAP (As Many Reps As Possible). The hero card highlights it. Use the rep stepper to enter your actual reps. If RPE logging is enabled, a purple stepper appears to record how hard the set felt (8 = 2 reps left, 9 = 1 rep left, 10 = absolute max).

If your settings include a Goal 1RM, a panel shows how many reps you'd need at this weight to hit your target.

Step 7 — Realization Day (Week 3 of Each Wave)

Realization sessions are different. Instead of 10 working sets, you get 4 ramp sets (warm-ups at 50%/60%/70%/75% TM) followed by 1 AMRAP set. The ramp sets have blue "RAMP" pills. Rate each ramp: Heavier than expected / Normal / Lighter than expected. Or tap "Quick Log All Warm-Up Sets" to skip all 4 in one tap.

After ramps, a coaching note appears based on your feedback. Then the AMRAP: this is your test. Every rep beyond the wave target earns you a 1RM increase (5 lb lower body, 2.5 lb upper body, capped at 10 extra reps).

Step 8 — Complete Session

Once every set is logged, the Complete Session button enables. Tap it.

The Session Complete overlay appears with:

Summary card — lift name, wave/phase, tonnage, session duration, AMRAP estimated 1RM, readiness score, sets completed. If it was a realization day and you earned a new 1RM, it's highlighted in green with the increase amount.

Session notes — a text area to record anything notable ("hip felt tight on set 3", "new belt felt great", "grip slipped on set 5"). Saved to your history.

AI Coaching Insight — if you have an Anthropic API key in Settings, a purple card loads with 2–3 sentences of personalized coaching based on your sets, RPE, readiness, movement quality, and volume trends. This takes a few seconds to generate.

"Where did you struggle?" — tap your sticking point: Out of the hole, Midrange, Lockout, Off the chest, Off the floor, or No issues. This selection does two things: it highlights matching accessories with a purple star badge, and it may trigger an accessory swap suggestion in a purple card below.

Accessory swap suggestion — if your current T1 accessory doesn't target the weakness you just selected, and a better option exists in the library, the app suggests the swap: "Consider swapping Pause Squat → Close-Grip Bench to target your lockout weakness." One tap on Apply Swap executes it. If you've reported the same sticking point 2+ sessions in a row, the suggestion is emphasized.

Step 9 — Continue to Accessories

Tap "Continue to Accessories →" on the completion overlay. The app transitions to inline accessory logging — no tab switch needed. Each accessory card appears with:

Exercise name, tier label (T1/T2/T3), category, weight, rep range, and set steppers. If the exercise needs equipment you've toggled off in Settings, an amber warning appears.

For each set, tap the + / − buttons to enter your reps. When every set in an exercise hits the top of the rep range (e.g. all 4 sets hit 8 reps on a 5–8 range), a green progression glow appears with the suggested next weight.

Tap the ? on any exercise to see coaching cues (setup, execution, common mistakes), a Watch Tutorial YouTube link, the equipment type, and swap pills — tappable alternatives filtered by your available equipment, each showing a tag like (BB), (DB), (BW).

You can also swap exercises from the tier list at the top of the Accessories tab — currently selected exercises glow green, tap any other pill to swap it in with one tap.

Step 10 — Finish & Review

Tap "Finish Full Session" when accessories are done. You're returned to the dashboard. The lift chip now shows a green checkmark. The Weekly Plan updates — one fewer session due.

Tap the History tab to see your session log: weekly summary with tonnage and readiness comparison arrows, 1RM sparklines (green), volume sparklines (blue), readiness trend (amber), and individual session entries with outcome badges (PR / Full / Managed / Deload).

If you completed all 4 lifts this week, the program automatically advances to the next phase. The timeline bar updates — another green pill.

If You Need to Leave Mid-Workout

Close the app at any point during a session. Your progress is saved automatically. Next time you open the app, a resume prompt appears showing the lift, set progress, and readiness score — tap "Resume" to pick up exactly where you left off.

Installing as an App (PWA)

This app can be installed on your phone's home screen for a full-screen, app-like experience with no browser bar.

iPhone (Safari): Tap the Share button (box with arrow) → scroll down → "Add to Home Screen." The app icon will appear on your home screen with a green "J" logo.

Android (Chrome): Tap the three-dot menu → "Add to Home Screen" or "Install App."

Once installed, the app includes a service worker that caches the page for offline use. You can open and use the app without an internet connection — your data is stored locally in IndexedDB.

Resuming Unfinished Sessions

If you close the app mid-workout (battery dies, phone call, etc.), your session is preserved. The next time you open the app, a prompt appears showing the lift, set progress, and readiness score, and asks if you want to resume. Tap "Resume" to pick up exactly where you left off, or dismiss to start fresh.
